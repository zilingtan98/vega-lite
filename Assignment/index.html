Simple Guide to Understand Your Choice of Wines.

What is Wine? 🍷

Wine is an alcoholic beverage made with the fermented juice of grapes.

They are made of grapes, but not like the ones you bought in the grocery stores! Wine grapes are usually smaller, sweeter, contains seeds and mostly have thick skins. There are over 1300 wine grape varieties used to in commercial production but only about 100 of these varieties make up 75-80% of the world's vineyards. The most common wine grape used in the world is Cabernet Sauvignon.

Types of Wine

Wines generally can be categorized into seven different types—Red, White, Dessert, Rosé, Sparkling, Fortified and Port/Sherry. These types of wines are introduced as they are suitables for different type of dishes such as chicken, pork, cured meat, lamb, beef, barbecue, Italian, American, Chinese, Thai, Indian, full-flavored like cheddar and firm cheeses such as Manchego.

Despite wine's global popularity, its production is a fairly exclusive industry. Four countries—France, Italy, Spain, and the United States—produce more than half the wine in the world.

Pricing of Wine

There are a few factors that could determine the cost of a wine, such as type of grapes used, wine origin region, taxation, winemaking process, and labor cost.

Port/Sherry wines generally are more expensive than other types of wines, as it is stored longer and fortified. It costs a lot of money to keep the product in a shed for several years before it is blended and bottled. Also, because of the higher alcohol content, typically 20% ABV, which attracts a higher level of taxation.

On the other hand, Rosé wines are cheaper to make, spends almost no time in the cellar, and is released early. Rosé wine's cost of production is among the lowest of all categories of wine.

Comparison Between Wine Types

Low Alcohol Wines: Under the 10% ABV level, most wines will be light in body and sweet. Usually these are Sparkling wines.

Medium-Low Alcohol Wines: Ranging from 10–11.5% ABV are usually produced when less-sweet grapes are used to make wine. Common in White and Sparkling wines.

Medium Alcohol Wines: Ranging from 11.5%–13.5% ABV is the average. Common in White wines.

Medium-High Alcohol Wines: Ranging from 13.5%–15% ABV. Average dry wines such as Red and Rosé wines usually falls in this category.

High Alcohol Wines: Ranging from 15%–23% ABV. Made one of two possible ways: naturally or with fortification. Usually fortified so that wine was to preserve the flavor of wines during the age of exploration. Dessert, Fortified, and Port/Sherry wines usually fall under this category.
